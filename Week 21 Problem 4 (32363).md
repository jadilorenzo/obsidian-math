$$
\begin{align*}  
x &= vt \cos \theta, \\  
y &= vt \sin \theta - \frac{1}{2} gt^2,  
\end{align*}
$$
$$
\begin{align*}
v(t) &= vt \sin \theta - \frac{1}{2} gt^2\\
&\dots \\
v'(t) &= v \sin \theta - gt = 0\\
v \sin \theta &= gt\\
\frac{v \sin \theta}{g} &= t\\
\end{align*}
$$

The peak is at $t=\frac{v\sin \theta}{g}$. This means the second $x$-intercept is at $2\frac{v \sin \theta}{g}$
